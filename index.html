HEARTHLIGHT
keep the fire · grow the light

Three pairs — six of you — slipped off the registry before dawn and ran for the unmapped wood. Behind you: a regime that rations the very light. Ahead: the dark, and the things it sends looking.

So you lit the one thing they cannot read: a fire. Feed it embers and the light grows — new ground, new crafts, new runaways drawn in from the dark. Let it gutter, and the watchers with glass eyes edge closer.

Nothing will be explained that you can discover. A year passes in half an hour. The fire never stops being hungry.
